Note:- The Factorial of a positive integer n is defined as the product of all positive integers from 1 to n. For example, 5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120. However, the factorial of a negative integer has no defined meaning, because the product of negative integers would never end. For instance, -1! would imply multiplying -1 by -2, -3, -4, and so on, which is impossible. Therefore, -1 factorial is undefined
