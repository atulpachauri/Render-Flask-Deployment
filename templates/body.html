Note:- A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that has exactly two distinct positive divisors: 1 and itself. This means it cannot be formed by multiplying two smaller natural numbers. For example, the numbers 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, and 13 are all prime numbers. Prime numbers play a crucial role in number theory and have various applications in fields such as cryptography
